
#  **Project Title**
**Customer & Sales Performance Analysis Dashboard**

##  **Project Background**
Understanding customer behavior and sales distribution is essential for businesses looking to improve performance and expand into new markets. However, without proper visualization, it becomes difficult to identify top customers, track sales patterns, and analyze geographic performance.

This project was developed to analyze customer purchasing behavior, sales distribution, and regional performance. The aim was to provide a clear view of where revenue is coming from and which customers contribute the most to overall sales.

## **Project Overview**
This dashboard provides insights into customer activity and sales performance across different regions and time periods.
It enables users to:
* Identify top customers based on quantity ordered
* Analyze customer distribution across countries
* Track sales performance geographically using a map
* Compare trends across different years (2017–2020)

The dashboard supports quick decision-making by presenting both customer-level and regional insights in a simple and interactive format.

##  **Key Insights / Key Points**

###  1. Top Customers Drive a Large Share of Sales
* A small group of customers contributes significantly to total quantity ordered
* Indicates dependency on key customers

###  2. Uneven Geographic Sales Distribution
* Sales are concentrated in specific regions (e.g., North America shown prominently)
* Some regions show low or minimal activity, indicating untapped markets
  
###  3. Customer Base Spread Across Countries
* Customers are distributed globally, but not evenly
* Highlights opportunities for regional expansion

### 4. Yearly Performance Tracking
* The presence of year filters (2017–2020) allows trend comparison
* Helps identify growth patterns or periods of decline
  
###  5. Opportunity for Customer Segmentation
* Current view focuses on quantity and location
* Suggests potential to further segment customers (e.g., by value, frequency)

##  **Recommendations**
###  1. Reduce Dependency on Top Customers
* Develop strategies to grow mid-level customers
* Reduce risk associated with losing key clients

### 2. Expand into Low-Performing Regions
* Investigate regions with low sales
* Introduce targeted marketing or partnerships
  
###  3. Strengthen High-Performing Markets
* Invest more in regions already generating strong revenue
* Improve customer retention strategies

###  4. Improve Customer Segmentation
* Segment customers based on:
  * Purchase frequency
  * Revenue contribution
  * Location
* Enables more targeted decision-making

###  5. Track Trends for Strategic Planning
* Use year-over-year comparisons to:
  * Forecast demand
  * Plan inventory
  * Adjust sales strategies
